Assignment: Optimization and Decision Models
1. Genetic Algorithm (GA)
Description: A search heuristic inspired by Charles Darwin’s theory of natural evolution. It evolves a population of candidate solutions toward an optimal result using three main operators: Selection (choosing the fittest), Crossover (combining parents), and Mutation (introducing random changes).
Examples:
Logistics: Optimizing the shortest delivery route for a fleet of vehicles (Traveling Salesman Problem).
Engineering: Designing high-performance airfoil shapes for aircraft wings to minimize drag.

2. Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO)
Description: A computational method that optimizes a problem by iteratively improving a candidate solution based on social behavior. "Particles" move through a search space by following their own personal best historical position and the global best position found by the entire swarm.
Examples:
Machine Learning: Optimizing the weights and biases of a Neural Network when traditional gradients are unavailable.
Power Systems: Managing the distribution of electricity across a grid to minimize energy loss.

3. Markov Decision Process (MDP)
Description: A mathematical framework for modeling decision-making in situations where outcomes are partly random and partly under the control of a decision-maker. It is defined by States, Actions, and Transition Probabilities, forming the foundation of Reinforcement Learning.
Examples:
Robotics: A vacuum robot deciding its next move (Forward, Turn, Stop) based on sensor data and room layout.
Finance: Determining optimal stock trading strategies where market movements are probabilistic.
